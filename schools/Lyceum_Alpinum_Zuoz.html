Details

The Lyceum Alpinum Zuoz, founded in 1904, is a Swiss international boarding school set in spectacular alpine landscape near St. Moritz. About three hundred students between 12 and 18 years of age from around the world and the Engadin valley follow programmes leading to the International Baccalaureate Diploma (English), the Swiss Matura (German, German/English or German/Italian) and the German Abitur.

Since young people started studying here over 100 years ago, it has always been truly international. Today the Lyceum Alpinum Zuoz is home to students of more than 30 different nationalities, and its alumni are studying at some of the world’s leading universities or pursuing their careers across the globe.

The Lyceum Alpinum Zuoz combines both respect for tradition and innovativeness. The "Spirit of Zuoz" is driven by five key concepts, which also define the academic and pastoral aims of education at the school: „Learning for Life“, „Self-Discipline“, „Commitment“, „Fair Play“ and „Cosmopolitanism“.

Founded in 1923, membership of the Alumni Zuoz Club is open to former students of the Lyceum Alpinum Zuoz. There are now more than 2500 members in 57 countries. The club has about 20 regional groups worldwide. The Zuoz Club offers its members the unique opportunity of remaining in life-long contact with the Lyceum Alpinum Zuoz irrespective of geographical distance or nationality. «Old Boys» and «Young Girls» from all over the world reunite twice a year to celebrate «Summer Games» and «Winter Games», which are devoted to sport, culture and close friendship.
